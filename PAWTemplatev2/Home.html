About our project

The Pangolin Automated Watch (PAW) - A Population Monitoring System for the Philippine Pangolin by Team Maharlika. Our goal is to monitor the pangolins in a conserved area using unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVâ€™s) that is connected to a cross platform system maintained by artificial intelligence that will keep track of trespassing poachers that will try to capture pangolins for their scales or meat.

Problem statement

The Philippine Pangolin is one of the endangered species here in the Philippines. Our goal is to have them monitored for their safety by harnessing the power of technology.

significance

The Pangolin Automated Watch is built by Filipinos to help conserve the environment by keeping track of the Philippine pangolin and warding off potential local and internation poachers that hunt them for their scales or meat that then will be sold to other neighboring countries.

  • Built by Filipinos

    The team behind the Pangolin Automated Watch are all Filipinos that are hoping to help conserve the environment with the help of the Pangolin Automated Watch.

  • Carefully Organized

    The project is coded with comprehensive programming languages and is developed and designed properly. It will use ArcGIS for mapping the location of the habitat of pangolin, TensorFlow for the model prediction, and Firebase for linking real time data for our website.

  • For the Philippine Pangolin

    The team behind the Pangolin Automated Watch is making sure that our project is feasible for the conservation of the Philippine pangolin in its natural habitat in the forests of Palawan.
